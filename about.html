About Me

I am a researcher and writer with a deep-rooted passion for Japanese language and culture. My academic work is driven by a fascination with the ways in which language, culture, and emotion intersect, particularly within the context of Japanese linguistics.

My research interests include Japanese grammar (bunpō), Classical Japanese (bungo), conceptual semantics, cognitive linguistics, philosophy of language, and the development of Japanese writing systems. In my doctoral thesis, I explore how emotion is encoded and shaped through Japanese language and cultural practices.

Beyond academia, I’m drawn to literature, short fiction, poetry, anime, and manga. I find joy in discovering cultural references that are subtle, layered, and poetic, and I enjoy making them accessible to others. One especially moving experience was being entrusted with a personal haiku collection by a palliative care patient of my spouse. That encounter continues to shape how I understand both language and human connection.

Living in Japan has offered me the rare opportunity to engage with these cultural and linguistic layers firsthand. Each day brings a new perspective, and I remain humbled by the depth and diversity of Japanese culture.

I would love to hear your story too. Feel free to reach out to me. I look forward to connecting.
